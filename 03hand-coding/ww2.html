Who Were the Leaders?

Most Famous WWII Leaders

Allied Leaders

Winston Churchill (United Kingdom)
Prime Minister known for his inspiring and defiant speeches during Britain's "darkest hour."
Franklin D. Roosevelt (United States)
President who led the U.S. through the Great Depression and most of the war, establishing the Lend-Lease Act.
Joseph Stalin (Soviet Union)
The leader of the Soviet Union during the war.
Charles de Gaulle (France)
Leader of the Free French forces who organized the French Resistance from exile.
Dwight D. Eisenhower (United States)
Supreme Commander of the Allied forces in Europe and mastermind of the D-Day invasion.

Axis Leaders

Adolf Hitler (Germany)
Dictator of Nazi Germany, responsible for initiating World War II in Europe and the systematic persecution and murder of millions.
Benito Mussolini (Italy)
The Fascist dictator of Italy, whose regime allied with Germany.
Emperor Hirohito (Japan)
The Emperor of Japan during the war.
Hideki Tojo (Japan)
The Prime Minister of Japan during much of the war.
Erwin Rommel (Germany)
Field Marshal and commander of the Afrika Korps, involved in campaigns in North Africa.
