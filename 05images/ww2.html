Who Were the Leaders?

Most Famous WWII Leaders

Allied Leaders

  1. Winston Churchill (United Kingdom): Prime Minister known for his inspiring and defiant speeches during Britain's "darkest hour."
  2. Franklin D. Roosevelt (United States): President who led the U.S. through the Great Depression and most of the war, establishing the Lend-Lease Act.
  3. Joseph Stalin (Soviet Union): The leader of the Soviet Union during the war.
  4. Charles de Gaulle (France): Leader of the Free French forces who organized the French Resistance from exile.
  5. Dwight D. Eisenhower (United States): Supreme Commander of the Allied forces in Europe and mastermind of the D-Day invasion.

Axis Leaders

  1. Adolf Hitler (Germany): Dictator of Nazi Germany, responsible for initiating World War II in Europe and the systematic persecution and murder of millions.
  2. Benito Mussolini (Italy): The Fascist dictator of Italy, whose regime allied with Germany.
  3. Emperor Hirohito (Japan): The Emperor of Japan during the war.
  4. Hideki Tojo (Japan): The Prime Minister of Japan during much of the war.
  5. Erwin Rommel (Germany): Field Marshal and commander of the Afrika Korps, involved in campaigns in North Africa.
